### Rectal Prolapse

A condition where a part of the rectum protrudes from the anus, or when the rectum slides out through the anal opening. Women ages 50 and older have six times more risk than men.

**Three Types**

1. The entire rectum extends out of the anus
2. A part of the rectal lining is extended out through the anus
3. The rectum extends down but does not come out (internal prolapse)

**Symptoms**

Rectal prolapse involves the feeling of a bulge or the appearance of reddish-coloured mass that extends outside the anus. Initially occurs during/after bowel movements; over time may extend spontaneously and require manual repositioning.

**Five Causes**

1. Long-term straining during bowel movements
2. Chronic constipation or diarrhea
3. Weakening of anal sphincter
4. Earlier injury to anal/pelvic areas
5. Older age

**Diagnosis**

Involves medical history and anorectal examination. Tests include anal electromyography, anal manometry, ultrasound, colonoscopy, proctosigmoidoscopy, and proctography.

**Treatment**

Surgical repair through: abdominal repair (open approach), laparoscopic surgery, or robotically assisted surgery.

**Post-Treatment Prognosis**

Hospital stay averages 2-3 days. Recovery typically takes one month, requiring avoidance of straining and heavy lifting for 6 months.
