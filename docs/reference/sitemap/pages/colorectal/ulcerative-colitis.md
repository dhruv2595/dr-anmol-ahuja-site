### Ulcerative Colitis

Ulcerative colitis (UC) can be defined as an inflammatory bowel disease which usually causes irritation, inflammation, and ulcers in the lining of your large intestine, or colon.

**Risk Factors**

- **Age:** People of every age are prone to UC but usually it begins before the age of 30.
- **Race/ethnicity:** Often, whites have the highest risk of the disease but UC is not limited to any race.
- **Heredity:** If someone has a family history of the disease then that person is generally at a higher risk.

**Causes**

However, the exact cause of UC is unknown. Earlier, diet and stress were suspected as the causes, but according to a study these factors may aggravate but don't cause UC.

**Symptoms**

- Abdominal cramping
- Pain
- Diarrhoea
- Bleeding with bowel movements
- Fever
- Fatigue
- Weight loss

**Diagnosis**

1. Blood tests
2. Stool sample
3. Colonoscopy
4. Flexible sigmoidoscopy
5. X-ray
6. CT scan

**Treatment**

*Medical Treatment:* The objective of medical therapy is to improve a patient's quality of life. Initially, the most common therapy applied is of corticosteroids (steroid hormones) combined with anti-inflammatory agents.

*Surgical Treatment:* Surgery involves the elimination of UC that means the removal of the entire colon and rectum (proctocolectomy).

**Post-surgical Prognosis**

After surgery, the patient can expect five to six bowel movements a day and one at night.

**Long-term Follow-up**

There always a need to remain in touch with the doctor as a regular follow-up is important.
