### Mini Gastric Bypass/One Anastamosis Gastric Bypass
The Mini Gastric Bypass (MGB) is a short, simple, successful, reversible laparoscopic gastric bypass weight loss surgery. It has been recently renamed as One Anastomosis Gastric Bypass.

### Surgery Procedure
Performed under general anesthesia, laparoscopic approach with small incisions, camera insertion, and a two-step process: stomach reduction via stapling into a "long slender pouch" and intestinal loop attachment creating an "omega loop" to bypass the lower stomach and duodenum.

### Post-operative Care
- Hospital stay: 1-2 days
- Pain management prescribed
- Wound care instructions
- Avoid strenuous activity initially; walking encouraged
- Liquid diet 10-15 days, then soft diet
- Daily hydration
- Follow dietician's regimen
- Exercise per surgeon's instructions

### Advantages
- Weight loss: 5 to 10 kilograms a month in the first year
- Resolves obesity-related conditions
- Fully reversible unlike RYGB

### Disadvantages
- Greater nutritional deficiencies than regular bypass
- Lifelong physician follow-up required

### Risks and Complications
Anesthesia issues, bleeding, infection, GI tract leaks, deep vein thrombosis, dumping syndrome, bile reflux, gallstones, malnutrition, marginal ulcers.
