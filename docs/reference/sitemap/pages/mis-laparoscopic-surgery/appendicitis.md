### Main Heading
"Appendicitis is an inflammation of the appendix, a finger-shaped pouch that projects from your colon on the lower right side of your abdomen."

### Signs and Symptoms
Initial presentation involves "pain across the abdominal area" that progresses to McBurney's point. Common symptoms include:
- Progressively worsening pain
- Painful coughing or sneezing
- Nausea and vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Inability to pass gas
- Fever
- Constipation
- Loss of appetite

### Causes
"A blockage in the lining of the appendix that results in infection is the likely cause of appendicitis."

### Complications
Include ruptured appendix causing peritonitis and abscess formation requiring drainage intervention.

### Diagnosis
Testing may involve "blood tests, to check for infection" and imaging such as MRI, CT, or ultrasound scans.

### Treatment Options
Include surgery (appendectomy), abscess drainage, antibiotics, pain relievers, IV fluids, and liquid diet.
