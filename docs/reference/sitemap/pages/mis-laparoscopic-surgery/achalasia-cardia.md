### Definition & Pathophysiology
The page describes achalasia cardia as "one of the common causes of motor dysphagia" resulting from "damage to nerves in the food tube (oesophagus), preventing the oesophagus from squeezing food into the stomach."

### Primary Symptoms
- Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing)
- Food regurgitation
- Chest pain
- Coughing when lying down
- Aspiration risk
- Weight loss

### Treatment Options
The page details five approaches: medications (calcium channel blockers, nitrates), balloon dilation (60% effective for 1 year), myotomy surgery (70-90% success rate), POEM (newer endoscopic procedure), and Botox injections (3-month relief).
