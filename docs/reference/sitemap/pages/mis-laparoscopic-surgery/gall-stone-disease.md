### Overview
The gallbladder stores concentrated bile for fat digestion. Gallstones are crystalline masses formed from bile pigments, cholesterol, and calcium salts, categorized as either cholesterol stones (80% of cases) or pigment stones.

### Risk Factors
Women face higher risk than men; age 30-40 is common; obesity, rapid weight loss, hormone therapy, pregnancy, and birth control pills increase vulnerability.

### Symptoms
Severe abdominal pain (especially after fatty meals), back pain, shoulder pain, nausea, vomiting, and bloating. Some patients remain asymptomatic ("silent stones").

### Diagnosis Methods
Ultrasound, oral cholecystography, HIDA scan, CT, MRCP, ERCP, and blood tests.

### Complications
Bile duct blockage, jaundice, pancreatic swelling, empyema, gangrene, cholangitis, and gallbladder cancer.

### Treatment Options
Symptomatic management with pain medication and antibiotics, or surgical removal via laparoscopic cholecystectomy or open surgery.
