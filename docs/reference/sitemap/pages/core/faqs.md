## FAQs

### Surgery for Diabetes

**Q: Can diabetes be cured by surgery?**
A: Diabetes is a condition that affects blood sugar levels and causes many serious health problems if left untreated or uncontrolled. There is no cure for diabetes, but it can go into remission.

**Q: Is there a surgery for diabetes?**
A: In addition to behavioural and medical treatments, bariatric surgeries constitute a powerful option to ameliorate diabetes in patients affected by severe obesity.

**Q: Is surgery dangerous for diabetics?**
A: Patients with diabetes have a higher risk of cardiovascular disease. Patients with diabetes have a higher perioperative risk.

**Q: Is there surgery for diabetes 2?**
A: For severely obese patients with higher than normal blood sugar or pre-diabetic, bariatric surgery can reduce or eliminate the risk of developing Type 2 diabetes.

**Q: How does bariatric surgery affect diabetes?**
A: Bariatric surgery results in significant weight loss and remission of diabetes in most patients.

**Q: How does Roux en Y cure diabetes?**
A: Roux-en-Y gastric bypass is frequently reported to achieve glycemic control and even cure type 2 diabetes in obese patients.

**Q: Can you drink wine after gastric bypass?**
A: Avoid alcohol for the first six months after bariatric surgery. When you get permission to start drinking alcohol again, avoid carbonated beverages and sugary drink mixers.

### Colorectal Surgery

**Q: What is the recovery time for colorectal surgery?**
A: Most people who have colon cancer surgery recover without any problems. These people go home within 2-to-4 days.

**Q: What does colorectal surgery involve?**
A: A bowel resection is a surgery to remove any part of the bowel. This includes the small intestine, large intestine, or rectum.

**Q: How long is hospital stay for colon resection?**
A: Bowel resection requires general anaesthesia. You may stay in the hospital for 4 to 7 days or as long as 2 weeks after surgery.

**Q: What can I expect after colostomy surgery?**
A: You may have very loose stools in your colostomy bag for a while. In time your stools may become firmer, but they will be less solid than before your surgery.

**Q: Can you live a normal life without a colon?**
A: People can live without a colon, but may need to wear a bag outside their body to collect stool.

### Proctology Surgery

**Q: How many hours does colon resection surgery take?**
A: Colectomy usually takes between 1 and 4 hours.

**Q: Is diverticulitis surgery painful?**
A: When you have diverticulitis, the inflamed pouches most often cause pain in the lower left side of your abdomen.

**Q: What can I expect after colon resection surgery?**
A: Right after the surgery, you'll be able to drink fluids. Recovery from a laparoscopic surgery is faster than with an open resection.

**Q: How long is recovery from small intestine surgery?**
A: The usual length of stay is 5 to 7 days in the hospital. Your doctor may choose to keep you longer if complications arise.

### MIS & Laparoscopic Surgery

**Q: What is MIS in surgery?**
A: MIS stands for "Minimally Invasive Surgery." The objective of MIS surgery is to allow for faster recovery.

**Q: What surgeries can be done laparoscopically?**
A: Most intestinal surgeries can be performed using the laparoscopic technique. These include surgery for Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, diverticulitis, cancer, rectal prolapse and severe constipation.

**Q: How long does it take to recover from laparoscopic surgery?**
A: This is common. You should feel better after 1 to 2 weeks.

**Q: Can I lay on my side after laparoscopy?**
A: Laparoscopy is usually done under general anaesthesia. Generally, a small incision is made near the belly button.

**Q: How do you treat laparoscopic incisions?**
A: Laparoscopy incisions are typically very small and rarely have complications. You may wash the incisions with soapy water.

**Q: What is the chance of getting pregnant after laparoscopy?**
A: The overall pregnancy rate was 41.9% (18/43). 66.7% (12/18) and 94.4% (17/18) of the patients conceived within postoperative 3 months and 6 months, respectively.

**Q: What should you avoid after surgery?**
A: Fresh fruit and vegetables contain both nutrients and fiber, which are essential to healing during your recovery from surgery.

### Hernia

**Q: How can you check yourself for a hernia?**
A: Your doctor will check for a bulge in the groin area. Because standing and coughing can make a hernia more prominent, you'll likely be asked to stand and cough or strain.

**Q: What are the symptoms of a strangulated hernia?**
A: Nausea, vomiting or both; Fever; Sudden pain that quickly intensifies; A hernia bulge that turns red, purple or dark; Inability to move your bowels or pass gas.

**Q: How do you check for a hernia on a woman?**
A: Sometimes hernia swelling is visible when you stand upright; usually, the hernia can be felt if you place your hand directly over it.

**Q: Can a hernia cause back and leg pain?**
A: The pain may not just be in the area of the hernia; it can radiate to your hip, back, leg — even to the genitals.

**Q: What should you not do if you have a hernia?**
A: Lists prevention tips including not smoking and maintaining a healthy body weight.

### Weight Loss Surgery

**Q: Do I qualify for weightloss surgery?**
A: Lists criteria including your body mass index (BMI) is 40 or higher.

**Q: Can bariatric surgery be done twice?**
A: In some cases, a second surgery to repair — or redo — a gastric bypass may be appropriate.

**Q: How quickly do you lose weight after gastric sleeve surgery?**
A: Expect your weight loss to slow down after 18 months.

**Q: Can you drink alcohol after gastric sleeve surgery?**
A: Alcohol is not recommended after bariatric surgery. Alcohol contains calories but minimal nutrition.

**Q: Can you have a baby after weight loss surgery?**
A: You should wait 18 months before getting pregnant. It's safe to get pregnant after bariatric surgery — after your weight stabilizes.

**Q: Can u have a baby after gastric sleeve?**
A: For a safe and healthy pregnancy, you should wait at least 18 months for your weight to stabilize before getting pregnant.

**Q: How many calories a day do you eat after gastric bypass?**
A: For the first two months following surgery, your calorie intake should be between 300 and 600 calories a day.

**Q: What is the safest bariatric surgery?**
A: Gastric banding is considered the least invasive weight loss surgery and also the safest.

**Q: How long does it take for your stomach to heal after gastric sleeve surgery?**
A: Four to six weeks. This entire process aims to let the stomach heal and patients usually return to work and their normal routine four to six weeks after the surgery.

**Q: How much can your stomach hold after gastric bypass?**
A: After surgery, your stomach can only hold 2 to 4 tablespoons of food or drink. After about a year, it will expand to hold up to 16 tablespoons.

**Q: How long after pregnancy can you have weight loss surgery?**
A: Approximately 18 months. Most experts recommend waiting approximately 18 months after bariatric surgery before getting pregnant.

**Q: How can I maximize weight loss after gastric sleeve or bariatric surgery?**
A: Provides 3-step guidance on hydration, protein intake, and scheduling appointments.

**Q: Can gastric bypass cause kidney problems?**
A: Bariatric surgery usually has a beneficial impact on renal malfunction (proteinuria, glomerular filtration) in obese patients.
