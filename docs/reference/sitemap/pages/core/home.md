### Weight Loss Surgery
Bariatric / Weightloss surgery also known as metabolic surgery has proven to be the most robust solution for obesity. Apart from reducing excess body weight by 80 to 90 percent it also significantly resolve comorbidities like diabetes mellitus, hypertension, OSA (apnea), knee and backaches and hyperlipidemia.

### Laparoscopic Surgery
In this technique small indications of 0.5 to 1 cm are made on abdomen and surgery is done with special instruments. All kinds of abdominal surgeries like weight loss, hernia and even cancer surgeries can be performed using this technique. Laparoscopic surgery gives better and faster recovery.

### MIS & Laparoscopic Surgery
Laparoscopic or "Minimally Invasive" surgery is a specialized technique for performing surgery. In traditional "open" surgery the surgeon uses a single incision to enter into the abdomen. Laparoscopic surgery uses one or more 0.5-1cm incisions.

### Hernia
A hernia occurs when an organ or fatty tissue squeezes through a weak spot in a surrounding muscle or connective tissue called fascia. The most common types of hernia are inguinal (inner groin), incisional (resulting from an incision), femoral (outer groin), umbilical (belly button), and hiatal (upper stomach).

### Colorectal
Colorectal cancer, also known as bowel cancer, colon cancer, or rectal cancer, is any cancer that affects the colon and the rectum. Colorectal cancer symptoms depend on the size and location of the cancer. Some commonly experienced symptoms include changes in bowel habits, changes in stool consistency, blood in the stool and abdominal discomfort.

### Weight Loss Surgery (description)
Bariatric surgery/ Weight loss surgery includes a variety of procedures performed on people who have obesity. Weight loss is achieved by reducing the size of the stomach with a gastric band or through removal of a portion of the stomach or by resecting and re-routing the small intestine to a small stomach pouch.

### BMI Calculator Classifications
- Below 18.5 is considered Underweight
- 18.5 – 22.5 is Normal Body Weight
- 22.5 – 27.5 is Overweight
- 27.5 – 32.5 is Class I Obesity
- 32.5 – 37.5 is Class II Obesity
- Above 37.5 is Class III Obesity (Morbid Obesity)
- More than 50, is classified as Super Obesity
