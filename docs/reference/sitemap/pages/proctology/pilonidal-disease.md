### Definition
"Pilonidal disease is a chronic skin infection in the crease of the buttocks near the coccyx (tailbone)."

### Disease Characteristics
More prevalent in males than females; typically emerges between puberty and age 40. Risk factors include obesity and coarse body hair.

### Causes
Hair growth in the buttock cleft can lead to follicle infections, with hair potentially entering abscess cavities.

### Symptoms
- Small dimple or large painful mass
- Clear, cloudy, or bloody fluid drainage
- Redness, tenderness, and foul-smelling pus if infected
- Possible fever, nausea, or illness

### Disease Progression
Most patients experience an acute abscess episode initially. Following resolution, many develop a pilonidal sinus—a subcutaneous cavity with surface openings requiring surgical intervention.

### Diagnosis
Physical examination of the buttock area by a physician.

### Treatment Approach
Primary acute abscess management involves drainage via incision under local anesthesia. Complex or recurring cases require surgical excision or unroofing procedures.

### Post-Surgical Care
Wound maintenance requires keeping the area clean and hair-free, with shaving/hair removal every 2-3 weeks until age 30.
