### Definition & Condition Overview
"An anal abscess can be defined as a painful condition in which a collection of pus develops near the anus." An anal fistula is described as "the tunnel that runs from inside the anus" connecting infected glands to an abscess.

### Abscess Classification
Five types are listed: perianal, ischioanal, intersphincteric, supralevator, and horseshoe abscess, with perianal being most common.

### Fistula Types
Four categories mentioned: intersphincteric (most common), transsphincteric, suprasphincteric, and extrasphincteric (least common).

### Symptoms
Abscess symptoms include "throbbing and constant pain in the anal area," constipation, rectal discharge, and swelling. Fistula symptoms involve "anorectal pain," drainage, fever, and foul-smelling discharge.

### Treatment Options
- Fistulotomy (unroofing the tract)
- Fibrin glue injection
- Anal fistula plug
- Endoanal advancement
- LIFT procedure
- Seton placement (temporary intervention)

**Recovery:** Pain management, sitz baths, and constipation prevention are recommended.
