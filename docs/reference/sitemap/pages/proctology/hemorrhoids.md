### Definition
"Hemorrhoids commonly known as piles are swollen veins in the lower part of the anus and rectum."

### Types
- External hemorrhoids form near the anus; usually painless unless blood clots develop
- Internal hemorrhoids form within the anus beneath the lining

### Symptoms
- Bleeding during bowel movements
- Protrusion of skin during bowel movements
- Lumps and swelling in the anal region
- Leaking faeces

### Contributing Factors
Ageing, chronic constipation or diarrhoea, pregnancy, heredity, straining during bowel movements, anal intercourse, obesity, and heavy lifting

### Treatment Options

**Nonsurgical approaches** include sclerotherapy, infrared photocoagulation, and electrocoagulation, plus self-care measures like "Eating a high-fibre diet and drinking more water"

**Surgical methods:**
- Rubber Band Ligation
- Injection and Coagulation
- Hemorrhoid stapling
- Hemorrhoidectomy (complete tissue removal)
